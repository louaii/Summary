.CyberSecurity: 
The practice of ensuring confidentiality, integrity and availability of information by protecting networks, devices, people and data from unauthorized access or criminal exploitation

.Threat actor:
Any person or group who presents a security risk, this risk can relate to computers, applications, networks and data

.Security role:
_Protect against external and internal hosts
_Meets regular compliance
_Maintains and improves business productivity
_Reduce expenses
_Maintaining brand trust

.Security analyst responsibility:
_Protecting computers and networks systems
_Installing prevention software
_Conducting periodic security audits

.Playbook:
List of how to go through a certain detection and what the analyst needs to look in order to investigate those incidents

.Compliance:
is the process of adhering to internal standards and external negotiations and enables organizations to avoid fines and security breaches

.Security frameworks:
are guidlines used for building plans to help mitigate risks and threats to data and privacy

.Security Controls:
are safeguards designed to reduce specific security risks. They are used with frameworks to establish a strong security posture.

.Security Posture:
is an organization's ability to manage its defence of critical assets and data and react to change. A strong security posture leads to lower risk for the organization

.Internal threat:
can be current/former employee, an external vendor, or trusted partner who poses a security risk. At times, an internal threat is accidental

.Network security:
practice of keeping an organization network infrastructure secure from unauthorized access. This includes data, services, systems and devices that are stored in an organization's network

.Programming:
to create a specific set of instructions

.Cloud Security:
ensures that assets stored in the cloud are properly configured and access to those assets in limited to authorized users. The cloud is a network made up of a collection of servers that store resources and data inn remote physical location that can be accessed via internet. Cloud security is a subfield of cyber that focuses on the protection of data, apps, infrastructures of cloud

Phishing Types:
_Business Email Compromise(BEC): A threat actor sending email seems legitimate and from trusted source in order to obtain financial advantage
_Spear phishing: A malicious email targets a user or group that seems originated from trusted source
_Whaling: spear phishing to gain access of sensitive data
_vishing: the exploitation of electronic voice communication to obtain sensitive information or to impersonate a known source
_smishing: The use of text messages to trick users in order to obtain sensitive info or to impersonate a known source

Social engineering types:
_Social media phishing: A threat actor collects detailed information about their target from social media sites

_watering hole attack: A threat actor attacks a website frequently visited by a specific group of users

_USB baiting: 
A threat actor strategy calls leaves a malware USB stick for an employee to find and install unknowingly infect a network

_Physical social engineering:
A threat actor impersonates an employee, customer, or vendor to obtain unauthorized access to physical location

In a data breach:
